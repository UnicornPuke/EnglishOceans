How It Works

Water power heavily relies on moving water as well as the water cycle to harness energy. Water is harnessed using two main methods.


Water power be made by natural forces, the flow of river, as well as non-natural forces,dams.


In the flow of river method, a turbine gets spun by the flow of a river and spins the generator creating energy.


In a dam, the stored water on the top gets released, turning the turbine and later powers the generator. Some even have a lower reservoir to store water and pump it back up.


In conclusion, water power can be created in different ways by different forces.
